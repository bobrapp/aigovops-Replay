## 1. Diagnosis: What the current site does in 5 seconds

| Element | Current state | Issue |
|---|---|---|
| Headline | "Every AI interaction, cryptographically proven." | Poetic but slow. "Cryptographically" forces translation. Mechanism, not outcome. |

## 2. The fix: lead with outcome, demote mechanism

### Recommended headline pattern

**Outcome verb in headline. Mechanism in subhead. Stakes in the strip below.**

### Headline options by audience

| Audience | Headline | Subhead |
|---|---|---|
| Compliance / Risk (Expert mode) | Prove what your AI said. Every time. | Tamper-proof receipts for every AI chat — pass audits, catch policy violations, replay any conversation in one click. |
| Builders / Devs | The black box for your AI. | Signed, replayable receipts for every model call. Drop-in SDK, policy-as-code, full audit trail. |
| Journalists / Researchers (Simple mode) | Screenshots can be faked. Receipts can't. | Save every AI conversation as cryptographic proof — verify what was said, when, and by which model. |
| Punchy / general | Court-ready receipts for AI. | Every prompt and response, cryptographically signed and replayable. |

### Recommended pick (used in the prototype)

> # Prove what your AI said. Every time.
>
> Tamper-proof, cryptographically signed receipts for every AI conversation — pass audits, catch policy violations, and replay any chat in one click.

## 3. Replace feature bullets with stakes

The current three-bullet feature list explains the product. Replace with three **stakes** that explain the *consequences of not having it*.

| Stake | Copy |
|---|---|
| Disputed AI output? | Replay the exact conversation, byte-for-byte. Same prompt, same model version, same response — every time. |
| Auditor at the door? | Hand them a signed receipt for any AI interaction. Cryptographic proof, not screenshots that anyone can fake. |
| Policy violation? | Flagged the moment it happens. Policy-as-code rules check every interaction the instant it's signed. |

Pair each with a small icon. Each one is one beat — the eye scans them in ~1.5 seconds.

## 5. Fix the right rail

The mode picker + Replit sign-in + feedback popover all fight for attention before the visitor knows what the product is.

| Current | Recommended |
|---|---|
| Simple/Expert mode selector above the fold | Move below the fold, or behind the CTA. First-time visitors don't know which they are. |
| "Sign in with Replit" as primary action | Demote to secondary. Make **"See a sample receipt"** primary — zero clicks to the aha moment. |
| Feedback popover always visible | Suppress until the user has scrolled or spent >15s. It's currently the brightest button on the page. |

## 7. Trust signals in the corner of the eye

In 5 seconds, peripheral vision matters. Add a thin strip under the hero with:

- `SHA-256 signed` · `Immutable chain` · `Open source` · `Policy-as-code` badges.
- One customer or design-partner logo, even a pilot one.
- "Used to verify N receipts" live counter (if you have the data).

These don't need to be read — they register as legitimacy.
